# Contributing to OpenSCU
### Open Settlement Clearing Union

OpenSCU is an open, collaborative effort to design a neutral, asset-referenced settlement infrastructure.

We welcome contributors from multiple disciplines. This is not a typical software project—OpenSCU sits at the intersection of:

- Distributed systems  
- Economics and monetary design  
- Energy markets  
- Governance and institutional frameworks  

---

## Guiding Principles

Before contributing, understand the foundation:

- **Open by default** — All work is public  
- **Neutrality first** — No single actor dominates  
- **Substance over speculation** — This is infrastructure, not a token  
- **Clarity over complexity** — If it can’t be explained, it won’t be adopted  

---

## Where You Can Contribute

You do not need to do everything. Pick a lane.

---

### 🔧 Protocol & Systems Development

Focus:
- Settlement engine design  
- Distributed ledger architecture  
- Transaction validation logic  

Starter Tasks:
- Define basic settlement flow (trade → clearing → reconciliation)  
- Propose system architecture (modular vs monolithic)  
- Evaluate existing frameworks (e.g., permissioned vs public systems)  

---

### 📊 Economic & Monetary Modeling

Focus:
- Asset-referenced unit design  
- Stability mechanisms  
- Liquidity considerations  

Starter Tasks:
- Propose initial asset basket (oil, gas, etc.)  
- Define weighting methodology  
- Model volatility scenarios and stress cases  

---

### 🛢️ Energy & Commodity Analysis

Focus:
- Real-world asset validation  
- Pricing benchmarks  
- Supply dynamics  

Starter Tasks:
- Compare Brent vs WTI vs regional benchmarks  
- Define data sources for pricing  
- Outline verification methods for reserves and production  

---

### 🔗 Oracle & Data Systems

Focus:
- Reliable external data inputs  
- Price feeds and validation  
- Anti-manipulation mechanisms  

Starter Tasks:
- Identify trusted data providers  
- Design multi-source verification model  
- Propose fallback mechanisms for data outages  

---

### 🏛️ Governance Design

Focus:
- Decision-making frameworks  
- Voting mechanisms  
- Participation models  

Starter Tasks:
- Define proposal system (how changes are introduced)  
- Outline voting structure (who participates, how weighted)  
- Suggest dispute resolution mechanisms  

---

### 📚 Documentation & Research

Focus:
- Clarity and accessibility  
- Translating complex ideas into understandable formats  

Starter Tasks:
- Improve README and docs  
- Create diagrams and explanatory content  
- Summarize research into digestible formats  

---

## How to Start

### 1. Explore the Repository
Read:
- `/docs/vision.md`  
- `/docs/architecture.md`  
- `/docs/ucc-model.md` (working draft)  

---

### 2. Check Open Issues

Start with tagged issues:
- `good first issue`  
- `research`  
- `discussion`  

---

### 3. Open a Discussion First

Before building something large:

- Open an issue  
- Share your thinking  
- Get feedback early  

---

### 4. Submit Contributions

- Fork the repository  
- Create a branch  
- Submit a pull request  

All contributions should include:
- Clear explanation  
- Reasoning behind decisions  
- Trade-offs considered  

---

## Contribution Standards

To maintain quality and credibility:

- No anonymous major proposals  
- No speculative token discussions  
- No political positioning or agendas  
- All assumptions must be stated clearly  

---

## What We Are Building Toward

OpenSCU is not a short-term project.

We are working toward:

- A functional settlement model  
- A validated asset reference framework  
- A transparent governance system  
- A credible foundation for real-world adoption  

---

## Who Should Contribute

You don’t need to be an expert in everything.

You should contribute if you are:

- A builder who wants to work on real systems  
- An economist interested in alternative settlement models  
- An analyst with insight into energy or commodities  
- Someone who believes transparency should be foundational  

---

## Final Note

OpenSCU will only succeed if it earns trust.

Trust is not claimed.
It is built—through transparency, rigor, and collaboration.

If that resonates with you, you’re in the right place.

## Contribution Boundaries

To maintain clarity and integrity, all contributions must align with the following:

- OpenSCU is a **settlement system**, not a currency or token
- No speculative token mechanics or retail use cases
- No political positioning or alignment with any single state or bloc
- No unnecessary complexity—prefer simple, explainable models
- All proposals must clearly state assumptions and trade-offs

Contributions that do not align with these principles may not be accepted.
