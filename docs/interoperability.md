# Interoperability & System Compatibility

OpenSCU is designed as a neutral settlement layer that can interface with existing accounting, financial, and trade systems.

This document defines the requirements and evaluation framework for interoperability.

---

## Purpose

- Enable integration with external systems  
- Ensure consistency in settlement logic  
- Maintain transparency and auditability  

---

## Core Requirements

For a system to integrate effectively, it should support:

### 1. Transparent Accounting

- Clear tracking of value movement  
- Auditability of transactions  
- Consistent reporting standards  

---

### 2. Asset Referencing Capability

- Ability to map value to real-world asset benchmarks  
- Support for dynamic valuation inputs  

---

### 3. Data Accessibility

- Programmatic access (APIs or equivalent)  
- Reliable and timely data updates  

---

### 4. Settlement Clarity

- Defined processes for clearing and reconciliation  
- Visibility into final settlement states  

---

### 5. Neutral Operation

- No unilateral control over value issuance or validation  
- Resistance to single-actor manipulation  

---

## Compatibility Tiers

Systems may be evaluated across three categories:

---

### 🟢 Compatible

Meets core requirements:

- Transparent accounting  
- Accessible data  
- Clear settlement processes  
- Minimal central control over validation  

---

### 🟡 Partially Compatible

Meets some requirements but has limitations:

- Limited transparency  
- Restricted data access  
- Partial centralization  

**Notes should specify which requirements are not met.**

---

### 🔴 Not Aligned

Does not meet core requirements:

- Opaque accounting  
- Restricted or closed data  
- High degree of centralized control  
- Inability to map to asset-referenced valuation  

---

## Evaluation Metrics (Draft)

Each system may be evaluated across:

- Transparency score  
- Data accessibility score  
- Settlement clarity score  
- Degree of centralization  
- Asset referencing capability  

---

## Important Note

This framework is not intended to approve or reject systems.

It is intended to:

- Provide clarity on interoperability  
- Highlight strengths and limitations  
- Enable informed integration decisions  

---

## Open Questions

- How should metrics be quantified?
- Should scoring be standardized or contextual?
- How do we handle hybrid systems?
