# Contributing to OpenSCU

Thank you for your interest in contributing.

OpenSCU is an open, collaborative initiative—but it is also a **disciplined system design effort**. Contributions must align with the core purpose and principles outlined below.

---

## Core Direction

OpenSCU is:

- A settlement and clearing infrastructure  
- Asset-referenced (real-world inputs such as energy)  
- Open-source and transparent  
- Designed for institutional and regional use  

---

## Out of Scope

The following are not aligned with OpenSCU:

- Cryptocurrency or token design  
- Retail payment systems  
- Speculative financial mechanisms  
- Politically aligned proposals  

---

## Contribution Boundaries

All contributions must:

- Maintain neutrality (no single-actor control)  
- Prioritize simplicity over complexity  
- Be clearly explained and well-reasoned  
- Include assumptions and trade-offs  

---

## Proposal First

Before submitting major changes:

1. Open an issue  
2. Describe your idea clearly  
3. Gather feedback  
4. Then proceed with implementation  

Pull requests without prior discussion may be closed.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

A contribution may be accepted if it:

- Aligns with the project’s core purpose  
- Improves clarity, robustness, or usability  
- Demonstrates thoughtful reasoning  
- Does not introduce unnecessary complexity  

---

## Conduct

- Be respectful and constructive  
- Focus on ideas, not individuals  
- Keep discussions grounded and objective  

---

## Final Note

OpenSCU aims to build trust through transparency and rigor.

Not all contributions will be accepted—but all thoughtful contributions are valued.
